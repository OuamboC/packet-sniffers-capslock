What is Privileged Access Management and why should you care?

🔒 PAM Deep Dive · 5 min read · Security Fundamentals
Scroll to explore
Foundation

The gatekeeper for the keys to the kingdom

Your daily interactions online and at work are often shaped by Privileged Access Management. You may have experienced the effects — and sometimes the frustrations — without realising what's being controlled behind the scenes.

PAM makes sure that the most "powerful" accounts can only be used by the right people, at the right time, and only for the specific tasks they are allowed to perform.

It does this by controlling access, monitoring activity and shutting down anything that looks risky or unexpected.

If an attacker compromises a normal user account, the damage they can do is limited. But if they get hold of an admin account, they can take over everything. PAM prevents that escalation.

🚫
Bouncer for Dangerous Accounts
Domain admins, database admins, root and cloud super-users are guarded at the door.
⚖️
Enforces Least Privilege
Users get only the minimum access they need — nothing more, nothing less.
📋
Records & Audits Everything
Every privileged action is logged so you always know who did what, and when.
Simply put, PAM is the gatekeeper for the keys to the kingdom.

The most critical accounts in any organisation deserve the strictest protection.

Comparison

PAM vs IAM — What's the difference?

IAM and PAM sound similar but serve different purposes. Think of IAM as identity validation and PAM as resource access validation.

🏢
IAM — Reception Desk

Verifies who you are, issues you a badge, and determines which floors and rooms you can access. Applies to every employee, contractor, and system identity.

VS
🔐
PAM — The Vault Room

Biometric scanners, CCTV, and time-locked doors protect the most critical assets. A subset of IAM focused exclusively on elevated, privileged accounts.

Attribute IAM PAM
Scope All users across the organisation Only privileged / admin accounts
Goal Right people, right access, right time Secure and monitor elevated access
Functions Authentication, SSO, directory services, provisioning Credential vaulting, session recording, JIT access
Tools Microsoft Entra ID, Okta, Ping Identity CyberArk, BeyondTrust, Delinea, HashiCorp Vault
Compliance GDPR, ISO 27001, Cyber Essentials NIST 800-53 AC-6, ISO 27001 A.9, PCI DSS Req. 7 & 8
Analogy "Who are you and what can you access?" "You have the master key — let's watch and control how you use it."
Framework

The Three Pillars of PAM

People, Technology, and Process work together. None of these pillars work in isolation — all three must be in place for PAM to be effective.

01
👥
People
  • Admin security training
  • Separation of duties
  • Privileged user vetting
  • Clear escalation paths
  • Regular access reviews
02
⚙️
Technology
  • Password vaulting
  • MFA for privileged access
  • Session recording
  • Just-in-Time provisioning
  • Auto credential rotation
03
📝
Process
  • Joiners / Movers / Leavers
  • Quarterly recertification
  • Incident response plans
  • Change management
  • Compliance auditing
Real-World Scenario

PAM Workflow in Action

How PAM protects domain admin credentials through a Just-in-Time access model — no standing privileges, full monitoring.

1
No Standing Domain Admins

The Domain Admin Group is kept empty by default. There are no permanent domain admin accounts sitting idle and exploitable.

Zero Standing Privilege
2
Access Request via PAM Portal

The IT admin submits a formal access request through the PAM portal, specifying the task they need to perform and the duration required.

Approval Workflow
3
Temporary Access Granted

An ephemeral domain admin account is created. The session starts — "Admin Access for 45 Min" — tightly scoped to the specific task.

Just-in-Time Access
4
Session Monitoring

All activity is recorded and logged in real time. Automated alerts fire for any suspicious or anomalous actions during the session.

Full Audit Trail
5
Automatic Cleanup

When the session ends, the ephemeral account is automatically deleted and access expires. No residual credentials remain.

Auto Revocation
Key Takeaways

Why PAM matters

PAM allows organisations to give elevated permissions on a need-to-use basis only. This fundamentally limits the attack surface available to malicious actors.

A real-world PAM scenario: an IT admin needs temporary, highly privileged access to fix a critical problem — but the organisation wants to avoid giving them standing admin rights that could be compromised or abused.

With PAM, that admin gets exactly what they need, for exactly as long as they need it, under full observation — and nothing more.
